Patients always praised Sandra Hardy- Rogers was an amazing British lady, who was a whirlwind in the emergency department, caring for patients with a slightly rough-around-the-edges style that somehow made almost everyone smile. “There was nobody as good as her,” said Allyse Gombas, a colleague. About 5ft tall, Hardy-Rogers was “all spunk, all enthusiasm”.

Hardy-Rogers arrived in the United States as a young woman, working first as a nanny and then as a home health aide, then got a job at the hospital, eventually settling into an overnight shift in the emergency department. In late March, after she treated a patient tested positive, soonly she became sick and fought for about a month in the hospital’s intensive care unit before her organs shut down.
